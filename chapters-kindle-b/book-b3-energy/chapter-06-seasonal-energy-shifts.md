# Seasonal Energy Shifts

You've probably noticed that you feel different in different seasons — and not just because of the weather. There are seasons when you're unstoppable and seasons when you can barely function. Seasons when ideas flow and seasons when everything feels stuck. You might have chalked this up to sunlight, temperature, or mood. But Saju has a more specific explanation.

Each season carries a dominant elemental energy. And the interaction between that seasonal energy and your Day Master element creates a predictable effect — sometimes boosting you, sometimes challenging you, sometimes doing both at once.

---

## The Season-Element Map

- **Spring (February - April):** Wood energy. Growth, expansion, upward movement.
- **Summer (May - July):** Fire energy. Peak expression, heat, visibility.
- **Late Summer (August - September):** Earth energy. Transition, stabilization, harvest.
- **Autumn (October - November):** Metal energy. Contraction, refinement, release.
- **Winter (December - January):** Water energy. Rest, depth, conservation.

These aren't exact calendar dates — they're energetic windows that shift slightly each year. But the elemental quality of each season is consistent.

---

## How Each Season Affects Your Element

### Spring (Wood Season)

**Wood Day Masters in Spring:** This is your season. Wood energy amplifies Wood energy. You feel a surge of vitality, ambition, and forward motion. New projects start easily. Ideas come fast. The body feels flexible and eager to move. The risk: over-expansion. With so much Wood energy in the environment plus your own internal Wood, you can take on too much, commit to too many plans, and stretch yourself past sustainability. Spring is your launch window — but not everything launched in spring needs to be maintained through summer.

**Fire Day Masters in Spring:** Energized and inspired. Wood feeds Fire, so the seasonal energy directly nourishes your system. This is an excellent season for planning creative projects that will peak in summer. The energy you absorb in spring becomes the fuel for summer's fire.

**Earth Day Masters in Spring:** Challenged. Wood controls Earth in the elemental cycle, and spring's dominant Wood energy can feel overwhelming to Earth. You might experience restlessness, digestive disruption, or the unsettling feeling that everyone around you is moving faster than you are. This isn't a failure season — it's a season where Earth needs to consciously ground: routines, familiar foods, consistent sleep.

**Metal Day Masters in Spring:** Uncomfortable. Metal cuts Wood, and Wood resists Metal — so spring creates a friction between the seasonal energy and your internal system. You might feel irritable, critical, or out of sync with the general optimism around you. Accept that this is a lower-energy season and adjust expectations accordingly.

**Water Day Masters in Spring:** Pleasantly depleted. Water feeds Wood, so spring draws on your reserves to fuel the environmental growth energy. You might feel generous but tired — like you're contributing to something bigger but losing personal energy in the process. Moderate your giving. Keep reserves for yourself.

### Summer (Fire Season)

**Fire Day Masters in Summer:** Maximum power. Your element matches the season, and you feel it in every cell. Social energy peaks. Creativity blazes. You're magnetic, visible, and alive. The risk: burnout. All this Fire energy — internal plus external — can overheat the system. Schedule deliberate cool-down periods. Don't mistake peak intensity for sustainable intensity.

**Earth Day Masters in Summer:** Nourished. Fire produces Earth, so summer's energy feeds you directly. This is often Earth's best season — you feel warm, supported, and energetically full. Use this surplus wisely. Store it. Build things that will sustain you through autumn and winter.

**Wood Day Masters in Summer:** Drained but purposeful. You're feeding the seasonal Fire energy — your ideas, your projects, your plans become fuel for the summer's intensity. This can feel meaningful and exhausting simultaneously. Make sure you're not giving everything away. Keep some creative reserves for yourself.

**Water Day Masters in Summer:** Directly challenged. Fire evaporates Water, and summer is the season where Water people feel most vulnerable. Energy is low. Sensitivity is high. Social demands feel exhausting. Emotional regulation gets harder. This is not a "push through" season for Water — it's a "protect yourself" season. Seek shade, literal and metaphorical.

**Metal Day Masters in Summer:** Stressed. Fire melts Metal, and summer's heat — both physical and social — can feel corrosive to Metal's structured internal world. The casual, chaotic energy of summer parties and spontaneous plans disrupts Metal's need for order. Find your own rhythm within the season. You don't have to match everyone else's energy.

### Late Summer (Earth Season)

**Earth Day Masters in Late Summer:** Grounded and centered. The brief Earth season (late August through September) is your power window. Everything stabilizes. The frenetic energy of summer settles, and Earth's harvesting instinct activates. This is ideal for completing projects, consolidating gains, and strengthening relationships.

**Metal Day Masters in Late Summer:** Nourished. Earth produces Metal, so this transitional season feeds your system. The approaching autumn feels right — like the world is finally moving in your direction. Use this window to prepare for your own peak season ahead.

**All other elements in Late Summer:** A transitional pause. Neither boosting nor challenging — just a brief stabilizing moment before the energy shifts again.

### Autumn (Metal Season)

**Metal Day Masters in Autumn:** Your season. The air gets crisp, the light gets clear, and your entire system sharpens. Autumn Metal energy amplifies your natural precision, focus, and discernment. This is when Metal does their best analytical work, makes their clearest decisions, and feels most aligned with their own standards. The risk: over-cutting. With so much Metal energy available, the impulse to refine can become the impulse to eliminate. Be careful about what you decide to let go of during autumn — some of it might be worth keeping.

**Water Day Masters in Autumn:** Nourished. Metal produces Water, and autumn's energy directly feeds your system. As the world quiets and contracts, Water feels increasingly at home. Your reserves start filling. Your depth returns. Autumn is Water's pre-season — the preparation for winter's deep work.

**Wood Day Masters in Autumn:** Challenging. Metal cuts Wood, and autumn's energy can feel like active pruning. Projects get questioned. Confidence wavers. The growth you built in spring and summer faces scrutiny. This isn't destruction — it's refinement. The question autumn asks Wood is: "which of these things are worth continuing?" Let go of what isn't.

**Fire Day Masters in Autumn:** Declining. The Fire that blazed in summer is being controlled by autumn's Metal energy. Energy drops. Visibility decreases. The world feels less responsive to Fire's warmth. This is a natural cycle, not a crisis. Turn inward. Let the fire burn smaller and more focused.

**Earth Day Masters in Autumn:** Gently drained. Earth produces Metal, so you're feeding the seasonal energy. The nurturing instinct intensifies — you want to prepare others for the coming cold. Make sure you're preparing yourself too.

### Winter (Water Season)

**Water Day Masters in Winter:** Deep power. This is your season, and the energy available is profound. Winter is when Water accesses their deepest reserves — intuition sharpens, insight deepens, creative and emotional processing reaches its full capacity. The world is quiet, and Water thrives in the quiet. The risk: going too deep. Winter can pull Water into isolation, depression, or detachment from the physical world. Maintain some external connections even when every instinct says "go inward."

**Wood Day Masters in Winter:** Nourished but dormant. Water feeds Wood, and winter's energy is quietly preparing you for spring's growth. This doesn't feel productive — it feels like nothing is happening. But beneath the surface, the root system is expanding. Trust the dormancy. Spring is coming.

**Fire Day Masters in Winter:** Depleted. Water controls Fire, and winter is Fire's hardest season. Energy is low. Warmth is scarce — both physically and socially. The bright, expressive world that Fire thrives in goes gray and quiet. This is when Fire is most vulnerable to depression and emotional isolation. Actively seek warmth: heated spaces, warm people, bright environments, anything that counters the seasonal cold.

**Earth Day Masters in Winter:** Stable but cold. Earth isn't directly challenged by winter's Water energy, but the lack of Fire (Earth's feeder) means the energy supply chain is reduced. Earth may feel emotionally cooler than usual — less warm, less nurturing, more self-contained. This is normal. Restore through comfort practices.

**Metal Day Masters in Winter:** Transitioning. Autumn's peak has passed, and Metal is now feeding the seasonal Water energy. The precision that was so sharp in autumn softens slightly. This is a good time for Metal to rest — the year's most focused work is done, and the system needs to recharge.

## The Practical Application

Understanding your seasonal energy pattern means you can plan your year with it instead of against it:

- **Schedule launches, starts, and ambitious projects** during your element's strongest season
- **Schedule rest, reflection, and recovery** during your most challenging season
- **Prepare for predictable dips** instead of being blindsided by them
- **Don't compare your winter energy to someone else's summer energy** — seasonal timing is personal

The seasons change the energy landscape. Your chart is the map for navigating it.
