# Planning Your Year with Saju

You now have the framework. You understand the five relationships between your Day Master and the year's energy. You know how the Grand Fortune decade modifies everything. You can read the elemental signature of any year.

This chapter turns that knowledge into action — a practical system for making Saju-informed decisions about timing, strategy, and the year ahead.

## The Annual Check-In

At the start of every year (Lunar New Year is the traditional starting point, but the solar calendar works too), do this:

### Step 1: Identify the Year's Energy

Look up the Heavenly Stem and Earthly Branch of the coming year. Note:
- The Stem's element and polarity (Yang or Yin)
- The Branch's main element and hidden elements
- Whether the Stem and Branch harmonize or conflict

### Step 2: Find Your Relationship

Match the year's Heavenly Stem element to your Day Master. Which of the five relationships does it create?

- Same Element → Identity year
- Resource → Learning year
- Output → Creation year
- Wealth → Earning year
- Authority → Discipline year

### Step 3: Check the Grand Fortune Layer

If you know your current Grand Fortune decade (from a full chart reading), note which relationship IT creates with your Day Master. Then assess the stacking effect:

- Does the annual energy amplify the decade's energy? (Same direction = strong year)
- Does it counterbalance it? (Opposite direction = complex year)
- Is it neutral? (Different category = independent year)

### Step 4: Set Your Strategy

Based on the relationship and stacking, choose your annual approach:

**Identity Year (Same Element) → Differentiate**
- Clarify who you are and what you stand for
- Avoid herd mentality and competitive spirals
- Invest in personal brand and unique positioning
- Budget conservatively (Companion energy draws money outward)

**Learning Year (Resource) → Absorb**
- Pursue education, mentorship, and skill development
- Accept help and support without guilt
- Build knowledge that will fuel future production
- Don't expect big external results — this is a preparation year

**Creation Year (Output) → Produce**
- Start creative projects and bring ideas to life
- Express authentically — write, build, speak, create
- Budget for a high-expense year (creation costs money)
- Focus on two or three priorities, not twenty

**Earning Year (Wealth) → Capture**
- Pursue financial goals aggressively
- Negotiate compensation, close deals, acquire assets
- Monitor health — earning years are physically demanding
- Don't neglect relationships while chasing wealth

**Discipline Year (Authority) → Endure**
- Work within systems and meet external standards
- Build habits and discipline that outlast the pressure
- Prioritize health — Authority years stress the body
- Maintain your inner world even when the outer world demands compliance

## Seasonal Timing Within the Year

The annual energy isn't constant — it modulates through the months. Use the seasonal element cycle to time specific actions:

**Spring months (Wood energy: Feb-Apr)**
Best for: Starting new projects, making plans, setting direction.
Worst for: Finishing old things, cutting ties, making final decisions.

**Summer months (Fire energy: May-Jul)**
Best for: Launching publicly, marketing, social events, presentations.
Worst for: Deep analysis, solitary work, conservative financial decisions.

**Late Summer (Earth energy: Aug-Sep)**
Best for: Consolidating gains, strengthening systems, relationship maintenance.
Worst for: Starting new ventures, taking big risks.

**Autumn months (Metal energy: Oct-Nov)**
Best for: Pruning, refining, editing, quality improvement, financial review.
Worst for: Starting new creative projects, making emotional decisions.

**Winter months (Water energy: Dec-Jan)**
Best for: Reflection, planning, deep rest, strategic thinking, year-end review.
Worst for: Public launches, high-energy social events, major commitments.

Combining the annual element with the monthly element creates a nuanced timing strategy. A Wealth year's best earning months are often the ones where the seasonal element also supports your Wealth Star. A Creation year's most productive months are often the ones where the seasonal element feeds your Output Star.

## The Decision Framework

For any significant decision — career change, investment, relationship commitment, relocation — run it through this quick Saju filter:

**1. What year energy am I in?**
If it's an Authority year, be cautious about major independent moves. If it's a Wealth year, financial decisions are favored. If it's a Resource year, decisions about education and growth are timely.

**2. Does this action align with the year's energy or fight it?**
Starting a business during an Authority year fights the current. Starting a business during an Output or Wealth year rides it. This doesn't mean Authority-year businesses fail — it means they require more effort and face more external resistance.

**3. What season is it?**
Even within a favorable year, timing matters. Launching in spring (growth energy) vs. autumn (pruning energy) produces different results. Align the action with the seasonal momentum.

**4. What does my Grand Fortune decade say?**
If the decade supports this type of action, the annual energy is less critical — you have a ten-year tailwind. If the decade is challenging, you need the annual energy to be aligned or you're fighting two currents at once.

## What Saju Timing Is and Isn't

### What it is:
- A framework for understanding energy conditions
- A tool for optimizing timing and strategy
- A way to prepare for predictable challenges
- Context for making sense of why some years feel different

### What it isn't:
- Fate. Nothing in your chart is fixed. The energy creates conditions, not outcomes.
- An excuse for inaction. "It's not my year" is never a valid reason to stop working, growing, or trying. Every year has opportunities. Every year has strategies that work.
- A substitute for good judgment. Saju informs decisions — it doesn't make them. A bad business idea during a Wealth year is still a bad business idea.
- Fortune-telling. Saju doesn't predict events. It maps energy conditions. What you do within those conditions is entirely up to you.

## The Saju Planning Paradox

Here's the beautiful paradox at the heart of Saju timing: **the people who benefit most from understanding the cycles are the ones who would succeed anyway.**

Understanding your year energy doesn't give you an unfair advantage. It gives you *awareness*. The awareness that this year's pressure is temporary, not permanent. The awareness that this year's ease won't last forever, so capitalize on it now. The awareness that the frustration you're feeling isn't personal failure — it's elemental friction, and it will pass.

That awareness doesn't replace effort, talent, or good decisions. But it removes the confusion and self-blame that waste enormous amounts of energy. When you know the year is an Authority year and you're fighting the current, you stop asking "what's wrong with me?" and start asking "what's the best strategy for this energy?"

That shift — from self-blame to strategy — is worth more than any prediction.

## Your Year Starts Now

Wherever you are in the cycle — peak or valley, creation or consolidation, wealth or discipline — the energy is what it is. You can't change the year. You can change how you move through it.

Read the energy. Align your strategy. Trust the cycle. And remember: the best year of your life isn't the one with the most favorable energy. It's the one where you understood the energy you were in and made the most of it.
