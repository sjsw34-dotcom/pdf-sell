# Reading the Year's Element

Before you can understand how a year affects you, you need to know what the year is made of. This chapter teaches you to read the elemental DNA of any year — past, present, or future.

## The Ten Heavenly Stems

The Heavenly Stems are the ten elemental signatures that cycle through every decade. Each element appears twice — once in its Yang (active, outward) form and once in its Yin (receptive, inward) form.

| Stem | Korean | Element | Polarity |
|------|--------|---------|----------|
| 甲 (Gap) | 갑 | Wood | Yang |
| 乙 (Eul) | 을 | Wood | Yin |
| 丙 (Byeong) | 병 | Fire | Yang |
| 丁 (Jeong) | 정 | Fire | Yin |
| 戊 (Mu) | 무 | Earth | Yang |
| 己 (Gi) | 기 | Earth | Yin |
| 庚 (Gyeong) | 경 | Metal | Yang |
| 辛 (Sin) | 신 | Metal | Yin |
| 壬 (Im) | 임 | Water | Yang |
| 癸 (Gye) | 계 | Water | Yin |

The cycle repeats every ten years. If you know any year's Heavenly Stem, you can count forward or backward to find any other.

**Quick trick:** The last digit of the year tells you the Heavenly Stem.
- 0 → 庚 Metal Yang
- 1 → 辛 Metal Yin
- 2 → 壬 Water Yang
- 3 → 癸 Water Yin
- 4 → 甲 Wood Yang
- 5 → 乙 Wood Yin
- 6 → 丙 Fire Yang
- 7 → 丁 Fire Yin
- 8 → 戊 Earth Yang
- 9 → 己 Earth Yin

Memorize the digit, and you'll know the Heavenly Stem of any year in seconds.

### Yang vs. Yin: Why It Matters

The same element in Yang vs. Yin form creates noticeably different year-energy:

**Yang years** feel bigger, more external, more forceful. A Yang Wood year pushes growth aggressively — new ventures start, change is imposed from outside, things move whether you're ready or not. A Yang Fire year is loud, visible, dramatic — events happen publicly, emotions run high, the spotlight is on.

**Yin years** feel subtler, more internal, more strategic. A Yin Wood year grows quietly — the changes are real but less visible, happening underground. A Yin Fire year is warm but contained — creativity deepens, passions refine, the intensity is personal rather than public.

Neither polarity is better. But knowing which you're in helps you calibrate your approach. Yang years reward bold action. Yin years reward patient strategy.

## The Twelve Earthly Branches

The Earthly Branches cycle every twelve years and are famously represented by the twelve zodiac animals. But the animals are shorthand — the real information is the element(s) hidden inside each Branch.

|--------|--------|-------------|-----------------|
| 子 (Ja) | Rat | Water | Water |
| 丑 (Chuk) | Ox | Earth | Earth, Water, Metal |
| 寅 (In) | Tiger | Wood | Wood, Fire, Earth |
| 卯 (Myo) | Rabbit | Wood | Wood |
| 辰 (Jin) | Dragon | Earth | Earth, Water, Wood |
| 巳 (Sa) | Snake | Fire | Fire, Earth, Metal |
| 午 (O) | Horse | Fire | Fire, Earth |
| 未 (Mi) | Goat | Earth | Earth, Fire, Wood |

| 酉 (Yu) | Rooster | Metal | Metal |
| 戌 (Sul) | Dog | Earth | Earth, Metal, Fire |
| 亥 (Hae) | Pig | Water | Water, Wood |

Notice that many Branches contain *multiple* elements. This is why the Earthly Branch creates a more complex undercurrent than the single-element Heavenly Stem. A Dragon year isn't just Earth — it's Earth with Water and Wood hiding inside. That hidden complexity explains why some years feel contradictory on the surface.

## Putting Stem and Branch Together

Every year's full signature combines one Heavenly Stem with one Earthly Branch. The combination creates one of sixty unique year-energies.

**When Stem and Branch harmonize:**

A Fire Stem sitting on a Fire Branch (like Yin Fire + Snake) creates a unified, clear year-energy. The surface and the undercurrent match. What you see is what you get. These years have a coherent feel — intense, if Fire; expansive, if Wood; stable, if Earth.

**When Stem and Branch conflict:**

A Water Stem sitting on a Fire Branch creates internal tension within the year itself. The surface says "reflect, go deep, be still." The undercurrent says "express, be visible, take action." These years feel contradictory — and they often produce contradictory results. You might experience professional growth (Fire energy pushing visibility) while simultaneously feeling emotionally drained (Water energy demanding rest).

**When Stem and Branch are neutral:**

Some combinations are neither harmonious nor conflicting — they're simply coexisting. These years tend to feel balanced but unremarkable. The energy is present but doesn't strongly push in any direction.

## The Seasonal Layer Within Each Year

Beyond the year's overall Stem and Branch, each month within the year also carries its own elemental energy. The twelve months map roughly to the twelve Branches:

- **Spring months (Feb-Apr):** Wood energy. Growth activates.
- **Summer months (May-Jul):** Fire energy. Expression peaks.
- **Late Summer (Aug-Sep):** Earth energy. Stability consolidates.
- **Autumn months (Oct-Nov):** Metal energy. Refinement occurs.
- **Winter months (Dec-Jan):** Water energy. Rest and depth.

This means the year's energy isn't constant — it modulates through the seasons. A Metal year in the spring months has a different feel than a Metal year in the autumn months, because the seasonal and annual energies are either complementing or counteracting each other.

## How to Look Up Any Year

You now have the tools to decode any year:

**Step 1:** Find the Heavenly Stem using the last-digit trick.
**Step 2:** Find the Earthly Branch by counting the animal cycle (or using a lookup table — sajumuse.com provides this automatically).
**Step 3:** Note the Stem's element and polarity.
**Step 4:** Note the Branch's main element and hidden elements.
**Step 5:** Assess whether Stem and Branch harmonize, conflict, or coexist neutrally.
