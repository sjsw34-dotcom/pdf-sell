# The Wealth Cycles

Here's something that will change how you think about money: your financial life has seasons.

Not the metaphorical "tough times and good times" that everyone experiences randomly. Actual, structural, predictable seasons — periods when the energy of your chart makes earning easier, investing smarter, and wealth accumulation natural, followed by periods when financial caution is genuinely the best strategy.

In Saju, these seasons are called the Grand Fortune (대운 / Daewoon) — ten-year cycles that shift the elemental energy landscape of your life. And within each Grand Fortune, annual cycles add shorter-term variations.

Understanding these cycles doesn't make you a fortune teller. It makes you a financial strategist with a calendar.

## The Grand Fortune: Your 10-Year Financial Weather

Every person's life is divided into ten-year segments, each governed by a specific Heavenly Stem and Earthly Branch combination. This combination adds an elemental influence to your baseline chart — sometimes amplifying what you already have, sometimes introducing what you lack.

For finances, the critical question is: **what element does your current Grand Fortune bring, and how does it relate to your Day Master?**

### When the Grand Fortune Brings Your Wealth Star

This is the financial expansion window. When the ten-year cycle brings the element that your Day Master controls (your Wealth Star), earning opportunities increase. Money flows more freely. Business ventures gain traction. Investments perform.

This doesn't mean money falls from the sky. It means the energy environment is aligned for wealth creation. You still have to act — but your actions produce disproportionate returns compared to periods when the Wealth Star is absent.

**What to do during a Wealth Star decade:**
- Increase earning capacity aggressively. Take the risk. Ask for the raise. Launch the side business. The wind is at your back.
- Invest more actively. The timing favors growth.
- Build financial infrastructure (accounts, systems, habits) that will sustain you through leaner cycles.
- Don't confuse favorable conditions with permanent conditions. This decade will end. Prepare for the transition.

### When the Grand Fortune Brings Your Output Star

The Output Star (Eating God / Hurting Officer) represents creative and productive energy. When it dominates your decade, you're in a *creation* cycle — generating value, building skills, producing work. This is the decade before wealth, not the wealth decade itself.

**What to do during an Output Star decade:**
- Build. Create. Develop products, skills, intellectual property, and reputation.
- Don't expect immediate financial returns. The value you're creating will monetize in the next cycle.
- Invest in capability over income. The course, the apprenticeship, the creative project that doesn't pay yet — these are financially rational during an Output decade.

### When the Grand Fortune Brings Your Resource Star

The Resource Star represents support, education, and backing. A Resource decade often brings mentorship, educational opportunity, family support, or institutional backing that indirectly supports financial growth.

**What to do during a Resource Star decade:**
- Learn. Study. Accept help. This isn't a "go it alone" period.
- Build relationships with people who have what you need for the next cycle.
- Accept that you might earn less now while building the foundation for significantly more later.
- If you receive inheritance or family financial support during this period, it's not coincidence — it's the Resource energy manifesting.

### When the Grand Fortune Brings Your Authority Star

The Authority Star (Proper Officer / Seven Killings) represents structure, pressure, and external demands. Financially, this decade often involves career pressure, institutional obligations, or the kind of structured employment that provides stability but limits entrepreneurial freedom.

**What to do during an Authority Star decade:**
- Work within systems. This is a good decade for corporate careers, government positions, and structured employment.
- Pay off debt. Authority energy favors meeting obligations.
- Build financial discipline. The pressure of this decade can forge habits that serve you for life.
- Don't fight the structure. Working against Authority energy is exhausting and financially costly.

### When the Grand Fortune Brings Your Companion Star

The Companion Star (Rob Wealth / Friend) represents competition and peer energy. Financially, this decade often brings both opportunity through collaboration and risk through competition for the same resources.

**What to do during a Companion Star decade:**
- Partner strategically. The energy favors collaboration — but choose partners carefully.
- Protect existing wealth. Companion energy can mean others competing for what you have.
- Avoid lending large sums. The social pressure to share resources is high during this period.
- Differentiate. In a competitive environment, the person with the clearest unique value wins.

## The Annual Layer

Within each ten-year Grand Fortune, each year adds its own elemental influence. Think of the Grand Fortune as the climate and the annual cycle as the weather.

A Grand Fortune that brings your Wealth Star creates a decade-long tailwind for finances. Within that decade, individual years can be warmer or cooler:

- **A year that also brings Wealth energy:** Double tailwind. Maximum earning potential. Don't waste this year.
- **A year that brings Output energy:** Creative production within a wealth decade. Build things that earn.
- **A year that brings Authority energy:** Structured pressure within a growth decade. Work harder, earn more.
- **A year that brings Resource energy:** A learning year within a growth decade. Upgrade skills that increase earning capacity.
- **A year that brings Companion energy:** Collaborative opportunity within a growth decade. Partner for larger deals.

Conversely, a Grand Fortune that brings Authority energy (financial pressure) can be moderated by an annual Wealth Star — a single good year within a challenging decade. Don't miss it.

## The Transition Years

The most financially volatile moments aren't the peaks or valleys — they're the transitions between Grand Fortune cycles. When a new ten-year period begins, the entire energy landscape shifts.

If you're transitioning from a Wealth decade to an Authority decade, the financial ease you've been experiencing suddenly faces resistance. Income opportunities that were easy become harder. Spending habits that were sustainable become risky.

If you're transitioning from an Authority decade to a Wealth decade, the financial pressure that felt permanent suddenly lifts. Doors open. Opportunities appear. The change can be disorienting because you're still operating with caution when the environment now rewards expansion.

**The practical advice for transitions:**
- Anticipate the shift 1-2 years before it hits
- Adjust spending and risk tolerance gradually
- Don't make major financial decisions during the transition year itself — wait until the new energy stabilizes

## The Biggest Wealth Cycle Mistake

The most common financial error in a Saju context isn't bad investing or overspending. It's **misreading the season**.

Trying to aggressively grow wealth during an Authority decade is like planting in winter. The effort is heroic. The results are minimal. And the frustration creates financial decisions driven by desperation rather than alignment.

Conversely, playing it safe during a Wealth decade is like staying indoors during the only sunny week of the year. The caution is understandable. The opportunity cost is enormous.

The cycles don't guarantee outcomes. They create conditions. A farmer can't control the weather, but a farmer who plants in spring and harvests in autumn outperforms one who does the opposite — every single time.
