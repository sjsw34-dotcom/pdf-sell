## How to Find Your Life Stage

Every pillar in your natal chart carries a life stage. So does every Grand Fortune decade, every Annual Fortune year, and every Monthly Fortune period. The method for finding them is always the same.

**Step 1: Identify your Day Master element.**

Your Day Master is the Heavenly Stem of your Day Pillar. What matters for life stage calculation is its *element* — Wood, Fire, Earth, Metal, or Water.

For Chart A (born 1960, female), the Day Master is Yin Wood (乙). The element is Wood.

**Step 2: Look at the Earthly Branch in question.**

Each Earthly Branch has a fixed relationship with each element. This relationship determines the life stage. The reference table from Chapter 16 maps every element-branch combination to one of the twelve stages.

**Step 3: Read the stage.**

For Chart A, let us find the life stage for each natal pillar:

| Pillar | Branch | Life Stage for Wood DM |
|--------|--------|----------------------|
| Hour | 申 Monkey | Birth |
| Day | 卯 Rabbit | Prime |
| Month | 巳 Snake | Sickness |
| Year | 子 Rat | Sickness |

Notice what this reveals. The Day Pillar — the seat of Chart A's identity — sits at Prime, one of the strongest positions in the cycle. This suggests a person whose core self carries confident, established energy. The Hour Pillar shows Birth, indicating that her later years and inner world carry the freshness of a new beginning.

**A note on methodology.** In traditional practice, two methods exist for calculating life stages: the standard method and the reverse method. They produce different results and practitioners debate which is more accurate. For this book, we use the standard method consistently. If you study with a Korean Saju master, ask which method they prefer — both have long traditions behind them.

> The key insight is this: you do not have just *one* life stage. Each pillar tells a different part of the story. And when Grand Fortune shifts the Branch every decade, the life stage shifts with it.

## Life Stages and Career Timing

One of the most practical applications of life stages is career timing. Korean Saju masters have observed consistent patterns between certain stages and professional outcomes.

**The ascending arc — building momentum:**

| Stage | Career Energy |
|-------|--------------|
| Birth | New skills, fresh starts, entering a field |
| Bathing | Experimentation, risk-taking, unconventional moves |
| Crown | Growing confidence, public recognition begins |
| Prime | Core competence established, steady productivity |
| Peak | Maximum influence, leadership, highest output |

When your Grand Fortune decade falls in the ascending arc — particularly at Prime or Peak — you are operating with the wind at your back. This does not guarantee success, but it means the underlying energy supports ambitious moves. Promotions, business launches, and career pivots during these stages tend to gain traction.

**The descending arc — consolidating and releasing:**

| Stage | Career Energy |
|-------|--------------|
| Decline | Slowing pace, time to delegate and mentor |
| Sickness | Reduced drive, possible burnout, need for rest |
| Death | Endings, closing chapters, letting go of roles |
| Tomb | Preservation, storing wisdom, behind-the-scenes work |
| Extinction | Complete reset, stripping away old identities |

The descending arc is not failure. It is harvest season followed by composting. A Grand Fortune decade at Decline might be the perfect time to step back from frontline work and move into advisory roles. A decade at Tomb might suit writing a book, archiving knowledge, or managing assets rather than creating new ones.

**The renewal arc — preparing for what comes next:**

| Stage | Career Energy |
|-------|--------------|
| Conception | New ideas forming, not yet visible to others |
| Nurture | Incubation, gathering resources, quiet preparation |

These two stages often feel frustrating to ambitious people. Nothing seems to be *happening*. But Saju practitioners recognize this as the underground root phase — the period when the next cycle's foundation is being laid. Starting a business during Conception may feel premature, but the seed is real.

> **Practical rule:** Do not force major career launches during Extinction or Tomb. Instead, use those decades to study, reflect, and prepare. When Birth arrives in the next Grand Fortune, you will be ready to move — with clarity that only comes from having fully released the old cycle.

## Life Stages and Relationships

Relationships have their own timing, and life stages offer a lens for understanding when connection comes easily and when it requires more effort.

**Stages that favor new relationships:**

- **Birth** — Fresh energy attracts new people. There is an openness and curiosity that draws others in.
- **Bathing** — High social magnetism but also vulnerability. Relationships formed here can be intense and transformative, though not always stable.
- **Crown** — Growing self-assurance makes you attractive. Partnerships formed during Crown often have a healthy, balanced quality.

**Stages that deepen existing relationships:**

- **Prime** and **Peak** — These are stages of established power. Existing relationships can reach their fullest expression here. Marriages during Prime or Peak often feel solid and purposeful.

**Stages that test relationships:**

- **Decline** and **Sickness** — Energy is waning. Relationships that lack a strong foundation may struggle. Those that survive become tempered and resilient.
- **Death** and **Tomb** — These stages can coincide with endings — separations, divorces, or the natural conclusion of relationships that have run their course. This is not fate dictating that you *must* lose someone, but rather that the energy environment makes it harder to sustain connections that are not deeply rooted.

**Stages that reset the pattern:**

- **Extinction** through **Nurture** — The slate is being wiped clean. Old relationship patterns dissolve. New types of connection become possible. People often report that the relationships they form after an Extinction period feel fundamentally different from those that came before.

In Korean culture, matchmakers have traditionally considered life stages when evaluating compatibility. A person entering a Birth or Crown decade is seen as being in an expansive, connective phase — a favorable window for marriage. A person at Tomb or Extinction might be advised to wait, not because love is impossible, but because the energy is oriented inward rather than outward.

## Life Stages and Life Transitions

The most dramatic life transitions — the ones that feel like earthquakes — often follow a recognizable pattern in the life stage cycle.

**The classic transition sequence: Death → Tomb → Extinction → Conception → Birth**

This five-stage arc represents a complete transformation. It is the caterpillar dissolving inside the cocoon before emerging as something new. When you see this sequence spanning two or three consecutive Grand Fortune decades, you can expect a period of profound change.

Here is what each phase of the transition tends to look like:

1. **Death** — Something ends. A career, a marriage, a belief system, an identity you held for decades. It may feel sudden, but if you look back, the decline was already underway.

2. **Tomb** — The aftermath. You are processing what happened. Grief, reflection, withdrawal. Externally, things may look quiet. Internally, a reorganization is happening.

3. **Extinction** — The bottom. The old self is fully gone. This is the most disorienting stage because there is nothing left to hold onto — but also nothing left weighing you down.

4. **Conception** — The first spark of the new. An idea, a meeting, a feeling that something is beginning. It is fragile and not yet fully formed.

5. **Birth** — The new cycle begins in earnest. Energy returns. Momentum builds. The person who emerges from this sequence is often unrecognizable compared to the person who entered it.

Understanding this pattern does not make the difficult stages painless. But it provides context. When you are in Tomb, knowing that Extinction and then Conception lie ahead can transform despair into patience. The cycle always turns.

> Korean Saju masters sometimes say: "Do not cry at Death — cry at Decline. By the time Death arrives, the hardest part is already over. And by Extinction, the new life is already being prepared."

### The Early Peak (Age 1-10): Peak

Chart A enters life at the highest point of the cycle. Peak energy in childhood often manifests as a bright, precocious start — a child who seems to have natural confidence, who draws attention effortlessly. The Grand Fortune stem 戊 (Yang Earth) represents Direct Wealth, and the branch 寅 (Tiger) brings Rob Wealth energy. There is abundance here, but also competition for it.

In practical terms, this might mean being born into favorable circumstances — a supportive family environment, early advantages. But starting at Peak also means there is only one direction to go from here: down. This is not a curse. It is simply the nature of the cycle.

### The Long Descent (Age 11-50): Decline → Sickness → Death → Tomb

From age 11 onward, Chart A enters the descending arc. Each decade drops one stage lower:

- **Age 11-20 (Decline):** The effortless energy of childhood begins to fade. This is adolescence and early adulthood — a period where Chart A must begin working harder for results that once came naturally. The Eating God energy in the stem suggests creative expression as a coping mechanism.

- **Age 21-30 (Sickness):** Reduced vitality. This decade often correlates with struggles — health challenges, career frustration, or relationship difficulties. The Hurting Officer stem adds a rebellious, restless quality. Chart A may feel that conventional paths are not working.

- **Age 31-40 (Death):** An ending. The Friend stem (乙, same as the Day Master) and the Direct Seal branch suggest that this ending may relate to identity itself — a fundamental shift in who Chart A understands herself to be. A marriage ending, a career abandoned, a move to a new country. Something dies so that something else can eventually be born.

- **Age 41-50 (Tomb):** Withdrawal and storage. The Rob Wealth stem and Direct Wealth branch create an interesting tension — there is energy for accumulation, but the life stage says *store, do not spend*. This is a decade for consolidating resources, processing the losses of the previous period, and quietly preparing.

### The Turning Point (Age 51-60): Extinction

This is the bottom of the cycle. At age 51, Chart A hits Extinction — the absolute zero point where the old cycle has fully ended but the new one has not yet begun.

The Grand Fortune pillar 癸酉 brings Indirect Seal (stem) and Indirect Officer (branch). Indirect Seal represents unconventional learning and spiritual insight. Indirect Officer brings external pressure and discipline. Together with the Extinction life stage, this decade suggests a period of deep, possibly painful, transformation. Old beliefs fall away. Old identities dissolve.

But look at what comes next.

### The Rebirth (Age 61-80): Conception → Nurture → Birth

At age 61, Conception arrives. The Direct Seal and Direct Officer combination brings structured support and legitimate authority. After decades of descent, Chart A begins to feel the first stirrings of a new cycle. This is not yet a full resurgence — Conception is subtle, internal, barely visible from the outside. But the direction has changed.

At age 71, Nurture continues the quiet buildup. And at age 81, Birth finally arrives — a full new beginning. The Direct Officer stem and Eating God branch combine authority with creative expression. Chart A at 81, if health permits, may experience a late-life renaissance — a period of renewed purpose that draws on everything the previous decades taught her.

Chart A's life is essentially divided into two halves. The first half (age 1-50) is a long, gradual descent from Peak to Tomb — a half-century of decreasing external energy. The second half (age 51 onward) is the rebuilding phase — starting from nothing and slowly accumulating new momentum.

This pattern carries a powerful lesson. A person with this Grand Fortune arc might spend their twenties, thirties, and forties wondering *why everything feels so hard*. They might compare themselves to peers who seem to be ascending while they are descending. But from a Saju perspective, Chart A is not failing — she is composting. The richness of her early Peak energy is being broken down, transformed, and prepared for reuse.

When Conception arrives at 61, Chart A has something that people who peaked late often lack: the wisdom of having lived through a complete descent. The new cycle is built on foundations that were tested by Death and refined by Extinction.

This is why Saju practitioners say that the life stage cycle is not a judgment — it is a map. Knowing where you are on the map does not change the terrain, but it changes how you walk through it. A person at Tomb who understands they are in Tomb can make very different choices than a person at Tomb who thinks they are simply unlucky.
