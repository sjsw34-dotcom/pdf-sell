The Output Stars describe what you create. The Wealth Stars describe what you acquire. Now we turn to what *governs* you — the forces that shape, discipline, and sometimes crush.

The Authority Stars represent the elements that *control* your Day Master. In the Controlling Cycle, Metal chops Wood, Water extinguishes Fire, and so on. When this controlling energy appears in your chart as a person, an institution, or a circumstance, it becomes an Authority Star.

Authority is not inherently negative. A sword needs a whetstone. A river needs banks. A fire needs a hearth. Without control, energy scatters and dissipates. The question — as always in Saju — is balance. Is the authority proportional to the Day Master's strength? Or is it overwhelming?

The Indirect Officer has an alternative name that reveals its nature: **Seven Killings** — *Chilsal* (칠살 七殺). In the Sexagenary Cycle, the element that controls you at the same polarity sits exactly seven positions away — hence "seven killings." This name is dramatic, but the energy is real: the Indirect Officer does not ask you to comply. It *forces* you to transform.

## Indirect Officer (편관) — The Commander

The **Indirect Officer** — *Pyeongwan* (편관 偏官), also called *Chilsal* (칠살 七殺) — is authority at its most raw and demanding. It is the drill sergeant, the crisis, the market crash, the boss who expects the impossible. It does not care about your comfort — it cares about results.

### Core Nature

The Indirect Officer shares polarity with the Day Master, creating a same-frequency clash — like two Yang forces colliding. This is not the controlled restraint of different polarities; it is a direct confrontation. The Indirect Officer attacks your weaknesses mercilessly.

**Key characteristics:**
- **Intense pressure** — demands that push you beyond your perceived limits
- **Transformative force** — the pressure that turns coal into diamonds, or shatters it
- **Military energy** — discipline through hardship, achievement through endurance
- **Unpredictable authority** — bosses who are brilliant but volatile, systems that change without warning
- **Power through crisis** — the Indirect Officer thrives in emergencies and high-stakes situations

### The Indirect Officer in Life

A person with a strong Indirect Officer tends to experience:

- **High-pressure career environments** — military, emergency services, competitive industries, startups
- **Volatile authority figures** — bosses or mentors who alternate between demanding and inspiring
- **Rapid advancement through crisis** — promotions that come during difficult periods, not comfortable ones
- **Physical and mental toughness** — the body and mind are forged by constant challenge
- **Difficulty with peaceful periods** — when pressure lifts, the person may feel restless or create drama

In career, Indirect Officer people gravitate toward: military, law enforcement, surgery, competitive sports, crisis management, turnaround consulting, and any field where high-stakes performance under pressure is valued.

### The Seven Killings: When Authority Becomes Destruction

The name "Seven Killings" is not metaphorical. When the Indirect Officer is too strong relative to the Day Master, the controlling energy does not refine — it destroys:

- **Health damage** — chronic stress, burnout, stress-related illness
- **Career destruction** — being fired, demoted, or pushed out by overwhelming institutional pressure
- **Psychological suppression** — feeling controlled, trapped, or unable to assert the self
- **Relationship intensity** — partnerships dominated by power dynamics and control

The antidote to excessive Indirect Officer is the **Eating God** (식신). As we learned in Chapter 10, the Eating God is the Output Star with same polarity. It *controls* the Indirect Officer through the counter-control principle: Output controls Authority (because Output produces Wealth, and Wealth produces Authority, creating a redirect of energy). In practical terms, creative expression defuses authoritarian pressure. This is why many people under intense career stress find relief through art, cooking, or other Eating God activities.

## Direct Officer (정관) — The Executive

The **Direct Officer** — *Jeonggwan* (정관 正官) — is authority at its most structured and legitimate. It is the corporate promotion, the government appointment, the recognition of your competence by established institutions. It is power that comes through proper channels.

### Core Nature

The Direct Officer has different polarity from the Day Master, creating a complementary control — Yin controlling Yang or Yang controlling Yin. This is not a collision but a coupling: the authority *fits* the person. The Direct Officer brings structure that supports rather than suppresses.

**Key characteristics:**
- **Institutional recognition** — titles, positions, formal authority
- **Structured advancement** — clear career paths, merit-based promotions
- **Social respectability** — the Direct Officer represents conventional success and public reputation
- **Disciplined growth** — consistent improvement within established frameworks
- **Spousal energy** — in traditional reading for women, the Direct Officer represents the husband — stable, responsible, conventional

### The Direct Officer in Life

A person with a strong Direct Officer tends to experience:

- **Clear career trajectory** — corporate ladder, academic progression, government ranks
- **Institutional trust** — the person is promoted because they are reliable, not flashy
- **Public reputation** — seen as responsible, competent, and trustworthy
- **Conventional relationship patterns** — traditional partnerships, defined roles
- **Difficulty with chaos** — when structures collapse, the Direct Officer person struggles more than most

In career, Direct Officer people gravitate toward: civil service, corporate management, law, academia, accounting, and any field where hierarchical advancement and institutional credibility matter.

### Direct Officer and the Hurting Officer Clash

Recall from Chapter 10 that the Hurting Officer specifically *hurts* the Direct Officer. When both are present in a chart, a fundamental tension exists:

- The Direct Officer wants structure, convention, and institutional legitimacy
- The Hurting Officer wants to break rules, challenge authority, and create through disruption

A person with both must navigate this internal contradiction: part of them craves stability and recognition, while another part rebels against the very institutions that provide it. The resolution often comes through finding environments that *reward* unconventional thinking within a structured framework — innovative divisions within large companies, academic positions with creative freedom, or entrepreneurial ventures with institutional backing.

### Reading the Authority Pattern

**Fire controls Metal.** The Yang Metal Day Master (庚, the Sword) is being melted by Fire from three directions: Yin Fire (丁) in the Hour Stem, and two Snake branches (巳) packed with Fire hidden stems. The sword is in the furnace.

**The Indirect Officers dominate.** Two out of three Authority Stars are Indirect Officers (편관/칠살) — the intense, forge-like pressure. This is not gentle institutional guidance; it is transformative force applied relentlessly.

**The Day Master has minimal support.** Only 辛 (Yin Metal, Rob Wealth) in the Year Stem provides companionship. No Resource Stars (Earth, which produces Metal) appear in the stems. The sword is being melted with barely any reinforcement.

**The Eating God provides relief.** 亥 (Pig, Water) in the Hour Branch is the Eating God for this Yang Metal Day Master (Metal produces Water, same polarity). As we discussed, the Eating God counters the Indirect Officer. This single element in the Hour Pillar — the domain of aspirations and later life — suggests that creative expression and flowing output become the escape valve from authority pressure.

### Life Pattern for Chart F

This chart produces a young man (born 2001, now in his mid-twenties) who:

- Faces intense external pressure — from family expectations, educational demands, or early career challenges
- Experiences authority as *transformative* rather than supportive — the pressure is designed to forge, not to coddle
- Has a strong rebellious streak (Hurting Officer 癸 in Month Stem clashes with Direct Officer 丁 in Hour Stem)
- May struggle with conventional career paths but thrive in high-pressure, competitive environments
- Finds relief through creative output (Eating God in Hour Branch) — this becomes increasingly important as life progresses

The Useful God for this chart is **Metal (金)** — strengthening the Day Master so it can withstand the Fire pressure without breaking. When fortune cycles bring Metal or Earth (which produces Metal), the sword hardens rather than melts.

> Chart F illustrates a crucial principle: authority pressure is not about what happens *to* you — it is about what it makes *of* you. The same fire that destroys weak metal tempers strong metal into the finest blade.

## Authority Stars and Other Gods

**Authority + Resource = The Mentor Chain.** Authority Stars produce Resource Stars (the element that controls you also produces what produces you, one step removed). When both are present, authority figures become teachers — the demanding boss who also mentors, the strict parent who also educates.

**Authority + Companion = Resilience.** When Authority Stars are counterbalanced by Companion Stars (same element as Day Master), the person can withstand pressure without breaking. The authority refines rather than destroys.

**Authority + Output = The Counter.** As discussed, Output Stars (especially the Eating God) counter the Indirect Officer. A chart with both creates a person who transforms pressure into creativity — the artist whose best work comes during the hardest times.

**Authority without Wealth = Powerless Power.** Authority Stars without Wealth Stars creates someone who holds positions of responsibility without the resources to fulfill them — the manager with no budget, the leader with no team. The title exists but the capacity does not.

## When Authority Stars Are Absent

A chart with no Indirect Officer and no Direct Officer means:

- The person has no natural relationship with conventional authority — neither drawn to it nor pressured by it
- Career advancement comes through other channels — talent (Output), money (Wealth), knowledge (Resource) — not through institutional climbing
- The person may struggle with discipline and structure — there is no internal compass for hierarchy
- Self-employment, freelancing, and creative careers feel more natural than corporate paths
- When Authority Stars arrive through fortune cycles, the experience can feel alien — sudden career structure or institutional attention that the person does not know how to navigate
