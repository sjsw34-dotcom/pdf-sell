The Output Stars describe what you create. The Wealth Stars describe what you acquire. Now we turn to what *governs* you — the forces that shape, discipline, and sometimes crush.

The Authority Stars represent the elements that *control* your Day Master. In the Controlling Cycle, Metal chops Wood, Water extinguishes Fire, and so on. When this controlling energy appears in your chart as a person, an institution, or a circumstance, it becomes an Authority Star.

Authority is not inherently negative. A sword needs a whetstone. A river needs banks. A fire needs a hearth. Without control, energy scatters and dissipates. The question, as always in Saju, is balance. Is the authority proportional to the Day Master's strength? Or is it overwhelming?

## The Two Faces of Authority

| Authority Star | Polarity | Korean | Character |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Indirect Officer** | Same as Day Master | 편관 偏官 / 칠살 七殺 | Intense, demanding, forge-like pressure |
| **Direct Officer** | Different from Day Master | 정관 正官 | Structured, legitimate, institutional authority |

The Indirect Officer has an alternative name that reveals its nature. *Chilsal* (칠살 七殺), or Seven Killings, refers to the fact that in the Sexagenary Cycle, the element that controls you at the same polarity sits exactly seven positions away. The name is dramatic, but the energy is real. The Indirect Officer does not ask you to comply. It forces you to transform.

## Indirect Officer (편관) — The Commander

The Indirect Officer, *Pyeongwan* (편관 偏官), also called *Chilsal* (칠살 七殺), is authority at its most raw and demanding. It is the drill sergeant, the crisis, the market crash, the boss who expects the impossible. It does not care about your comfort. It cares about results.

### Core Nature

The Indirect Officer shares polarity with the Day Master, creating a same-frequency clash, like two Yang forces colliding. This is not the controlled restraint of different polarities. It is a direct confrontation. The Indirect Officer attacks your weaknesses mercilessly.

The pressure is intense, pushing beyond perceived limits. It is a transformative force that turns coal into diamonds or shatters it. Military energy pervades, bringing discipline through hardship and achievement through endurance. Authority figures tend to be brilliant but volatile, and systems change without warning. The Indirect Officer thrives in emergencies and high-stakes situations.

### The Indirect Officer in Life

A person with a strong Indirect Officer tends to experience high-pressure career environments in military, emergency services, competitive industries, or startups. Authority figures are volatile, alternating between demanding and inspiring. Advancement is rapid but comes through crisis, with promotions arriving during difficult periods rather than comfortable ones. Physical and mental toughness develops as the body and mind are forged by constant challenge. When pressure lifts, the person may feel restless or create drama.

In career, Indirect Officer people gravitate toward military, law enforcement, surgery, competitive sports, crisis management, turnaround consulting, and any field where high-stakes performance under pressure is valued.

### The Seven Killings: When Authority Becomes Destruction

The name "Seven Killings" is not metaphorical. When the Indirect Officer is too strong relative to the Day Master, the controlling energy does not refine. It destroys. Health suffers through chronic stress, burnout, and stress-related illness. Careers can be destroyed by being fired, demoted, or pushed out by overwhelming institutional pressure. Psychological suppression sets in, leaving the person feeling controlled, trapped, or unable to assert the self. Relationships become dominated by power dynamics and control.

The antidote to excessive Indirect Officer is the Eating God (식신). As we learned in Chapter 10, the Eating God is the Output Star with same polarity. It controls the Indirect Officer through the controlling cycle. The element your Day Master produces (Output) directly controls the element that controls your Day Master (Authority). A Wood Day Master, for example, produces Fire (Output), and Fire controls Metal (Authority), so creative expression directly counteracts authoritarian pressure. This is why many people under intense career stress find relief through art, cooking, or other Eating God activities.

## Direct Officer (정관) — The Executive

The Direct Officer, *Jeonggwan* (정관 正官), is authority at its most structured and legitimate. It is the corporate promotion, the government appointment, the recognition of your competence by established institutions. It is power that comes through proper channels.

### Core Nature

The Direct Officer has different polarity from the Day Master, creating a complementary control, Yin controlling Yang or Yang controlling Yin. This is not a collision but a coupling. The authority fits the person. The Direct Officer brings structure that supports rather than suppresses.

Institutional recognition follows in the form of titles, positions, and formal authority. Career paths are clear, with merit-based promotions and structured advancement. The Direct Officer represents conventional success and public reputation. Growth is disciplined, with consistent improvement within established frameworks. In traditional reading for women, the Direct Officer represents the husband, someone stable, responsible, and conventional.

### The Direct Officer in Life

A person with a strong Direct Officer tends to experience a clear career trajectory through the corporate ladder, academic progression, or government ranks. Institutional trust follows because the person is promoted for being reliable, not flashy. Public reputation is strong, as the person is seen as responsible, competent, and trustworthy. Relationships tend toward conventional patterns with traditional partnerships and defined roles. When structures collapse, the Direct Officer person struggles more than most.

In career, Direct Officer people gravitate toward civil service, corporate management, law, academia, accounting, and any field where hierarchical advancement and institutional credibility matter.

### Direct Officer and the Hurting Officer Clash

Recall from Chapter 10 that the Hurting Officer specifically hurts the Direct Officer. When both are present in a chart, a fundamental tension exists.

- The Direct Officer wants structure, convention, and institutional legitimacy
- The Hurting Officer wants to break rules, challenge authority, and create through disruption

A person with both must navigate this internal contradiction. Part of them craves stability and recognition, while another part rebels against the very institutions that provide it. The resolution often comes through finding environments that reward unconventional thinking within a structured framework, such as innovative divisions within large companies, academic positions with creative freedom, or entrepreneurial ventures with institutional backing.

| **Relationship to self** | Forces transformation | Supports growth within bounds |

### Reading the Authority Pattern

Fire controls Metal. The Yang Metal Day Master (庚, the Sword) is being melted by Fire from three directions. Yin Fire (丁) sits in the Hour Stem, and two Snake branches (巳) are packed with Fire hidden stems. The sword is in the furnace.

Two out of three Authority Stars are Indirect Officers (편관/칠살), representing intense, forge-like pressure. This is not gentle institutional guidance. It is transformative force applied relentlessly.

The Day Master has minimal support. Only 辛 (Yin Metal, Rob Wealth) in the Year Stem provides companionship. No Resource Stars (Earth, which produces Metal) appear in the stems. The sword is being melted with barely any reinforcement.

亥 (Pig, Water) in the Hour Branch is the Eating God for this Yang Metal Day Master (Metal produces Water, same polarity). As discussed, the Eating God counters the Indirect Officer. This single element in the Hour Pillar, the domain of aspirations and later life, suggests that creative expression and flowing output become the escape valve from authority pressure.

### Life Pattern for Chart F

This chart produces a young man (born 2001, now in his mid-twenties) who faces intense external pressure from family expectations, educational demands, or early career challenges. Authority is experienced as transformative rather than supportive, as the pressure is designed to forge, not to coddle. A strong rebellious streak emerges from the Hurting Officer 癸 in the Month Stem clashing with the Direct Officer 丁 in the Hour Stem. Conventional career paths may feel like a poor fit, while high-pressure, competitive environments bring out the best performance. Relief comes through creative output (Eating God in the Hour Branch), which becomes increasingly important as life progresses.

The Useful God for this chart is Metal (金), strengthening the Day Master so it can withstand the Fire pressure without breaking. When fortune cycles bring Metal or Earth (which produces Metal), the sword hardens rather than melts.

> Chart F illustrates a crucial principle. Authority pressure is not about what happens to you. It is about what it makes of you. The same fire that destroys weak metal tempers strong metal into the finest blade.

## Authority Stars and Other Gods

Authority Stars produce Resource Stars (the element that controls you also produces what produces you, one step removed). When both are present, authority figures become teachers. The demanding boss also mentors. The strict parent also educates. This is the Mentor Chain.

When Authority Stars are counterbalanced by Companion Stars (same element as Day Master), the person can withstand pressure without breaking. The authority refines rather than destroys.

As discussed, Output Stars (especially the Eating God) counter the Indirect Officer. A chart with both creates a person who transforms pressure into creativity, the artist whose best work comes during the hardest times.

Authority Stars without Wealth Stars creates someone who holds positions of responsibility without the resources to fulfill them. The manager has no budget. The leader has no team. The title exists but the capacity does not.

## When Authority Stars Are Absent

A chart with no Indirect Officer and no Direct Officer means the following.

- The person has no natural relationship with conventional authority, neither drawn to it nor pressured by it.
- Career advancement comes through other channels such as talent (Output), money (Wealth), or knowledge (Resource), not through institutional climbing.
- The person may struggle with discipline and structure because there is no internal compass for hierarchy.
- Self-employment, freelancing, and creative careers feel more natural than corporate paths.
- When Authority Stars arrive through fortune cycles, the experience can feel alien, presenting sudden career structure or institutional attention that the person does not know how to navigate.
