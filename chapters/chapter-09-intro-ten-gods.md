You have spent Part 2 learning the language of elements — what they are, how they interact, and what your chart needs for balance. Now it is time to learn what the elements *mean* when they appear in specific positions relative to your Day Master.

This is the Ten Gods system — and it is where Saju transforms from an elemental analysis into a deeply personal reading of your life.

## What Are the Ten Gods?

The **Ten Gods** — known as *Sipsin* (십신 十神) — are ten relationship archetypes assigned to the elements in your chart based on their relationship to your Day Master. They are called "gods" not in a religious sense, but in the classical sense of *forces that govern specific domains of life*.

Here is the core insight: the same element — say, Fire — means completely different things in different charts. For a Wood Day Master, Fire is *output* (Wood produces Fire). For a Metal Day Master, Fire is *authority* (Fire controls Metal). For a Fire Day Master, Fire is a *companion* (same element). The element is the same; the relationship is different; the life meaning is different.

The Ten Gods system captures these relationships precisely.

## How to Identify Them

Every Ten God is determined by two factors:

**Factor 1: The element relationship** — There are five possible relationships between any element and your Day Master:

| Relationship | Meaning | Life Domain |
|---|---|---|
| Same element | Companion | Self, siblings, peers, competition |
| Day Master produces it | Output | Creativity, expression, children, talent |
| Day Master controls it | Wealth | Money, father, practical achievement |
| It controls Day Master | Authority | Power, discipline, career structure, husband (in traditional reading) |
| It produces Day Master | Resource | Knowledge, mother, mentorship, support |

**Factor 2: The polarity match** — Each relationship splits into two gods based on whether the polarity (Yin/Yang) matches:

| Polarity | Type | Character |
|---|---|---|
| Same polarity as Day Master | Indirect (偏) | Independent, unconventional, intense |
| Different polarity from Day Master | Direct (正) | Structured, conventional, stable |

Combining 5 relationships × 2 polarities = **10 Gods**.

## The Complete Ten Gods Table

| Relationship | Same Polarity (Indirect 偏) | Different Polarity (Direct 正) |
|---|---|---|
| **Companion** (same element) | **Friend** — *Bigyeon* (비견 比肩) | **Rob Wealth** — *Geopjae* (겁재 劫財) |
| **Output** (I produce) | **Eating God** — *Siksin* (식신 食神) | **Hurting Officer** — *Sanggwan* (상관 傷官) |
| **Wealth** (I control) | **Indirect Wealth** — *Pyeonjae* (편재 偏財) | **Direct Wealth** — *Jeongjae* (정재 正財) |
| **Authority** (controls me) | **Indirect Officer** — *Pyeongwan* (편관 偏官) | **Direct Officer** — *Jeonggwan* (정관 正官) |
| **Resource** (produces me) | **Indirect Seal** — *Pyeonin* (편인 偏印) | **Direct Seal** — *Jeongin* (정인 正印) |

This table is the single most important reference in Part 3. Bookmark this page — you will return to it constantly.

### The Naming Logic

Notice the pattern in the Korean names:
- **偏 (pyeon)** = "indirect" or "biased" — same polarity, unconventional
- **正 (jeong)** = "direct" or "proper" — different polarity, conventional

The indirect gods tend to be more dynamic, unpredictable, and independent. The direct gods tend to be more stable, structured, and socially accepted. Neither is better — they simply operate differently.

## The Five Pairs at a Glance

Before we dive deep into each god in the following chapters, here is a quick overview of what each pair governs:

### Companion Stars (비겁 — Friend + Rob Wealth)
**Domain:** Self-identity, siblings, peers, competition
**When strong:** Independent, competitive, strong sense of self
**When absent:** Isolated, lacking support, difficulty in team settings

### Output Stars (식상 — Eating God + Hurting Officer)
**Domain:** Creativity, expression, children, talent
**When strong:** Creative, expressive, prolific, sometimes scattered
**When absent:** Difficulty expressing ideas, blocked creativity

### Wealth Stars (재성 — Indirect Wealth + Direct Wealth)
**Domain:** Money, practical achievement, father
**When strong:** Financially oriented, practical, results-driven
**When absent:** Detached from material concerns, or money comes through other channels

### Authority Stars (관성 — Indirect Officer + Direct Officer)
**Domain:** Career, power, discipline, structure, husband (traditional)
**When strong:** Career-focused, structured, disciplined, sometimes pressured
**When absent:** Freedom-loving, difficulty with authority, self-employed tendency

### Resource Stars (인성 — Indirect Seal + Direct Seal)
**Domain:** Knowledge, mother, mentorship, support, education
**When strong:** Intellectual, well-supported, traditional learner
**When absent:** Self-taught, independent thinker, lacking conventional support

## Reading Your Ten Gods Distribution

The *distribution* of Ten Gods — which ones are present, which are absent, and which dominate — reveals the dominant themes of your life:

| Pattern | Life Theme |
|---|---|
| Output-dominant (식상 heavy) | A life centered on creation, expression, and talent |
| Wealth-dominant (재성 heavy) | A life centered on achievement, material success, and practical results |
| Authority-dominant (관성 heavy) | A life shaped by career, structure, and external expectations |
| Resource-dominant (인성 heavy) | A life centered on learning, mentorship, and intellectual growth |
| Companion-dominant (비겁 heavy) | A life centered on independence, competition, and self-reliance |
| Balanced distribution | A versatile life with multiple interests and adaptability |

Most charts have one or two dominant categories and one or two absences. A chart with *everything* present in equal measure is rare — and a chart with extreme concentration is where the most distinctive life stories emerge.
