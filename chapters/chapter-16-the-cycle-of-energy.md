# Part 4: Timing and Cycles

Welcome to the Master Edition.

If you are reading this chapter, you have decided to go deeper — past the foundations of Part 1, past the element dynamics of Part 2, past the Ten Gods personality system of Part 3. You now have a solid conceptual toolkit: you can read a chart's structure, identify its Day Master, map the Ten Gods, and understand element balance.

What you cannot yet do is read *time*.

Parts 1 through 3 treated the birth chart as a snapshot — a fixed map of energies present at the moment of birth. But Saju is fundamentally a system of cycles. The same chart that feels stuck at age 25 can feel electric at age 35, not because the chart changed, but because the *timing* changed. Parts 4 and 5 will teach you how to read those cycles.

We begin with the most elegant concept in the entire system: the Twelve Life Stages.

## 16.1 What Are the Twelve Life Stages?

The **Twelve Life Stages** — known as *Sibi Unseong* (십이운성 十二運星) — describe the lifecycle of energy in twelve phases. Think of them as a cosmic metaphor borrowed from human life: energy is born, grows, peaks, declines, rests, and is reborn. The cycle never ends. It simply repeats.

Here is a critical point before we go further: these stages are *not* literal predictions. A "Death" stage in your chart does not mean death. A "Sickness" stage does not mean illness. These are descriptions of how *strong or active* the Day Master's energy is within a particular pillar. "Death" means the energy has fully withdrawn — like a battery that has discharged and is ready to be recharged. Nothing more.

The twelve stages, in order.

| Stage | Romanization | Korean + Hanja | Energy State |
|---|---|---|---|
| Birth | *Jangsaeng* (장생 長生) | New energy emerges | Like a newborn — fresh, full of potential, not yet strong |
| Bathing | *Mogyok* (목욕 沐浴) | Energy is cleansed | Like a child's first bath — vulnerable, exposed, purifying |
| Crown | *Gwandae* (관대 冠帶) | Energy takes form | Like a young adult putting on formal dress — identity is declared |
| Prime | *Geollog* (건록 建祿) | Energy is established | Like a professional in their stride — competent, productive, stable |
| Peak | *Jewang* (제왕 帝旺) | Energy is at maximum | Like a ruler at the height of power — dominant but with nowhere to go but down |
| Decline | *Soe* (쇠 衰) | Energy begins to wane | Like the first gray hair — still strong, but the peak has passed |
| Sickness | *Byeong* (병 病) | Energy weakens | Like fatigue settling in — the body still functions, but momentum is lost |
| Death | *Sa* (사 死) | Energy is fully spent | Like deep sleep — outwardly still, but transformation is happening within |
| Tomb | *Myo* (묘 墓) | Energy is stored | Like a seed buried underground — hidden, concentrated, waiting |
| Extinction | *Jeol* (절 絶) | Energy is at zero | Like the pause between exhale and inhale — the absolute stillpoint |
| Conception | *Tae* (태 胎) | New energy is conceived | Like the moment of fertilization — invisible, but the cycle has restarted |
| Nurture | *Yang* (양 養) | Energy is nurtured | Like gestation — quietly growing, not yet ready to emerge |

After Nurture, the cycle returns to Birth, and the loop continues forever.

### Not Good or Bad — Just Strong or Quiet

One of the most common mistakes beginners make is treating the early stages (Birth through Peak) as "good" and the later stages (Decline through Extinction) as "bad." This misses the point entirely.

A chart loaded with Peak stages can describe someone who burns too hot — intense, dominant, but unable to rest or reflect. A chart with Tomb stages can describe someone with extraordinary depth — their energy is concentrated, stored, and released in powerful bursts when the timing is right. Tomb is actually one of the most interesting stages in professional interpretation, because it represents energy that is *compressed* rather than absent.

The Life Stages are a description of energy phase, not energy quality.

Chart D's Day Master is 壬 Yang Water. To determine the Life Stage for each pillar, we look at how the Day Master's energy behaves in each Earthly Branch. The traditional method assigns a stage based on the relationship between the Day Master element and the branch.

| Pillar | Branch | Life Stage | Energy Reading |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hour (丑 Ox) | Decline | Energy is waning — later life may require conservation and strategic focus |
| Day (寅 Tiger) | Sickness | Energy is weakened at the core — the self operates with quiet persistence rather than force |
| Month (午 Horse) | Conception | Energy is just being conceived — career and social life start from scratch and build slowly |
| Year (卯 Rabbit) | Death | Energy is fully spent — early life and family roots carry stillness, a sense of starting from deep rest |

### Reading the Pattern

What does this combination tell us? Chart D does not carry a single Birth, Crown, Prime, or Peak stage anywhere in her four pillars. Every stage falls in the second half of the cycle — the withdrawing, resting, and regenerating phases.

Does this mean Chart D is destined for a weak or difficult life? Not at all. It means her energy operates differently from someone whose chart is loaded with Peak and Prime stages. Her power is *internal*. It is the power of depth, patience, and regeneration.

The Month Pillar governs career and social life, and it carries the Conception stage. This placement suggests that Chart D's professional life does not arrive fully formed. It is conceived slowly, nurtured over time, and eventually born into something real. People with Conception in the Month Pillar often feel like late bloomers professionally, but what they build tends to be deeply rooted because it grew organically rather than being forced.

The Year Pillar carries Death — which, remember, is not literal. It describes the energy of her early life environment and ancestral influence as being in a "fully spent" phase. This can manifest as a childhood that felt quiet, contained, or disconnected from external support. The energy was resting, not absent. It was preparing for the next cycle.

The Day Pillar — the seat of the self — carries Sickness. This does not predict health problems. It describes a Day Master whose energy is naturally subdued, introspective, and careful. Yang Water is already the element of deep oceans and winter rivers. Combined with a Sickness stage, Chart D's self-expression is thoughtful rather than explosive. She observes before she acts. She conserves rather than spends.

### The Alternative Reading Method

It is worth noting that Saju tradition includes more than one method for assigning Life Stages. The method described above follows the standard assignment (*bongbeop* 봉법). An alternative method (*geobeop* 거법) sometimes produces different results — for Chart D, the alternative method yields Prime in the Year Pillar rather than Death, and different stages elsewhere. Which method a practitioner uses depends on their lineage and training. For this book, we follow the standard method, but you should be aware that variations exist.

### Why the Cycle Matters

The Twelve Life Stages give you something the Ten Gods alone cannot: a sense of *momentum*. The Ten Gods tell you what kinds of energy are present in your chart. The Life Stages tell you *how actively* that energy is flowing.

A Friend god sitting in a Peak stage pillar is a powerful, dominant companion energy. The same Friend god sitting in an Extinction stage pillar is a companion energy that is dormant — present in the chart's DNA but not actively expressing itself in that life domain.

This layering — Ten Gods for *what*, Life Stages for *how actively* — is what makes advanced Saju interpretation so much richer than beginner readings. In the coming chapters, we will apply this layered reading to the Grand Fortune cycles that govern the decades of your life.

## What You Have Learned

The Twelve Life Stages are a twelve-phase cycle describing how energy is born, strengthens, peaks, declines, rests, and is reborn. Every pillar in your chart carries a Life Stage assignment that reveals how actively your Day Master's energy operates in that life domain. The stages are not judgments — they are descriptions of phase. Decline is not failure. Death is not ending. Tomb is not burial. They are simply the quieter half of an endless cycle, the winter that makes the next spring possible.
