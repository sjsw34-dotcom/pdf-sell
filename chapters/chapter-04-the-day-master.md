If the Four Pillars chart is a stage, the Day Master is the main character. Everything else — the other stems, the branches, the Hidden Stems, the fortune cycles — exists in relation to this one character. Understanding your Day Master is the first and most important step in reading any Saju chart.

In this chapter, you will meet all ten Day Masters and learn how to assess whether yours is strong or weak. Then we will see how two people with the same Day Master can live very different lives — proof that Saju is far more nuanced than a simple ten-type personality system.

## What Is the Day Master?

The **Day Master** — *Ilgan* (일간 日干) — is the Heavenly Stem of your Day Pillar. It is the single most important character in your entire chart.

Why the Day Pillar? Remember from Chapter 2 that Xu Ziping, the Song dynasty master who perfected the Four Pillars system, deliberately shifted the interpretive center from the Year Pillar to the Day Pillar. His reasoning was profound: the day you were born is the most *personal* unit of time. Your birth year is shared with millions of people. Your birth month is shared with hundreds of thousands. But your birth day, combined with your hour, narrows the cosmic signature down to something uniquely yours.

The Day Master represents:

- **Your fundamental nature** — the instinctive way you respond to the world
- **Your core identity** — the self that remains constant even as circumstances change
- **The reference point** — every other element in the chart is interpreted in terms of its relationship to your Day Master

> Think of the Day Master as the sun in your personal solar system. The other elements are planets orbiting around it — sometimes supporting, sometimes challenging, but always defined by their relationship to the center.

## The 10 Day Masters: Complete Profiles

Each Day Master is defined by its element (Wood, Fire, Earth, Metal, Water) and its polarity (Yang or Yin). These two qualities together create ten distinct archetypes.

### Yang Wood 甲 (Gap) — The Tall Tree

**Element:** Wood | **Polarity:** Yang | **Image:** An ancient oak, standing tall and straight

Yang Wood people are natural leaders. Like a great tree, they grow upward — always reaching for something higher. They are principled, upright, and direct. When they believe in something, they stand firm, even when the wind pushes hard.

- **Strengths:** Integrity, leadership, ambition, straightforwardness, pioneering spirit
- **Challenges:** Rigidity, stubbornness, difficulty compromising, tendency to be inflexible under pressure
- **In relationships:** Protective, loyal, but can be controlling — they want to shelter others under their branches
- **Career inclinations:** Management, entrepreneurship, law, politics, architecture — fields that require vision and structural thinking

Yang Wood needs sunlight (Fire) and water to grow. When supported, they become towering presences. When unsupported, they become brittle and can snap under pressure.

### Yin Wood 乙 (Eul) — The Vine

**Element:** Wood | **Polarity:** Yin | **Image:** A climbing vine or a field of wildflowers

Yin Wood people are adaptable and resilient. Unlike the tall tree that breaks in a storm, the vine bends and finds another path. They are diplomatic, socially intelligent, and often underestimated — which they use to their advantage.

- **Strengths:** Flexibility, diplomacy, emotional intelligence, persistence, charm
- **Challenges:** Indecisiveness, dependency on others, difficulty standing alone, tendency to avoid confrontation
- **In relationships:** Warm, attentive, accommodating — but can become clingy or lose themselves in the relationship
- **Career inclinations:** Counseling, design, writing, teaching, human resources — fields that require connection and adaptability

Yin Wood thrives when it has something to cling to — a strong partner, a supportive team, a stable environment. In isolation, it struggles. But given any support at all, it grows in remarkable directions.

### Yang Fire 丙 (Byeong) — The Sun

**Element:** Fire | **Polarity:** Yang | **Image:** The blazing sun at midday

Yang Fire people light up every room they enter. They are warm, generous, optimistic, and impossible to ignore. Like the sun, they radiate energy outward — they give freely and attract others naturally.

- **Strengths:** Charisma, generosity, optimism, expressiveness, natural warmth
- **Challenges:** Impulsiveness, difficulty with subtlety, burnout, tendency to overwhelm others
- **In relationships:** Passionate, devoted, demonstrative — but can be dramatic and need constant appreciation
- **Career inclinations:** Performance, media, public speaking, hospitality, education — fields that put them in the spotlight

Yang Fire does not discriminate — the sun shines on everyone equally. This makes them beloved but sometimes naive about who deserves their warmth.

### Yin Fire 丁 (Jeong) — The Candle

**Element:** Fire | **Polarity:** Yin | **Image:** A candle flame or a campfire

Yin Fire people have an intimate, focused warmth. Unlike the sun that illuminates everything, the candle lights only what is close. They are perceptive, intuitive, and deeply thoughtful — they see details that others miss.

- **Strengths:** Perception, intuition, focus, sensitivity, intellectual depth
- **Challenges:** Moodiness, overthinking, jealousy, difficulty letting go, emotional intensity
- **In relationships:** Deeply devoted but possessive — their love is like a flame that can warm or burn
- **Career inclinations:** Research, psychology, technology, art, detective work — fields that require insight and focus

Yin Fire needs fuel (Wood) to sustain itself. Without purpose or passion to feed on, the flame flickers and fades.

### Yang Earth 戊 (Mu) — The Mountain

**Element:** Earth | **Polarity:** Yang | **Image:** A great mountain, solid and unshakable

Yang Earth people are the most stable and reliable of all Day Masters. They are grounded, patient, and trustworthy. Like a mountain, they do not move for anyone — which makes them both a fortress and an obstacle.

- **Strengths:** Stability, reliability, patience, tolerance, groundedness
- **Challenges:** Stubbornness, slowness to change, passivity, tendency to stagnate
- **In relationships:** Dependable and nurturing, but can be emotionally flat — they show love through action, not words
- **Career inclinations:** Real estate, agriculture, construction, management, government — fields that value consistency

Yang Earth contains hidden treasures (Metal is born from Earth), but you have to mine deep to find them. They reveal their depth slowly.

### Yin Earth 己 (Gi) — The Garden Soil

**Element:** Earth | **Polarity:** Yin | **Image:** Rich garden soil, fertile and nurturing

Yin Earth people are warm, accommodating, and productive. They provide the ground in which others grow. They are the caretakers, the supporters, the ones who make things possible behind the scenes.

- **Strengths:** Nurturing, practical, accommodating, productive, modest
- **Challenges:** Self-doubt, people-pleasing, difficulty saying no, tendency to absorb others' problems
- **In relationships:** Devoted and selfless, but can lose their identity — they give until there is nothing left
- **Career inclinations:** Healthcare, food industry, childcare, social work, agriculture — fields that nurture growth

Yin Earth is the most underestimated Day Master. They seem ordinary, but nothing grows without soil. Their value becomes apparent when they are absent.

### Yang Metal 庚 (Gyeong) — The Sword

**Element:** Metal | **Polarity:** Yang | **Image:** A forged sword or an axe

Yang Metal people are sharp, decisive, and action-oriented. They cut through problems with directness that others find either refreshing or terrifying. They value justice, efficiency, and results.

- **Strengths:** Decisiveness, courage, directness, justice-mindedness, competitiveness
- **Challenges:** Harshness, insensitivity, aggression, difficulty with nuance, tendency to create conflict
- **In relationships:** Loyal and protective, but can be domineering — they want to fix problems rather than listen
- **Career inclinations:** Military, surgery, finance, law enforcement, engineering — fields that require precision and resolve

Yang Metal must be tempered by Fire to become useful. Without challenges and refinement, the sword stays dull. Adversity literally makes them stronger.

### Yin Metal 辛 (Sin) — The Jewel

**Element:** Metal | **Polarity:** Yin | **Image:** A polished gemstone or fine jewelry

Yin Metal people are refined, aesthetic, and keenly sensitive. They notice quality — in people, in environments, in ideas. They have exacting standards and a natural elegance that draws attention.

- **Strengths:** Refinement, aesthetic sense, precision, eloquence, attention to detail
- **Challenges:** Perfectionism, vanity, sensitivity to criticism, emotional fragility, demanding nature
- **In relationships:** Romantic and attentive, but high-maintenance — they expect the same care they give
- **Career inclinations:** Art, luxury goods, beauty, finance, quality control — fields that demand aesthetic judgment

Yin Metal needs to be polished to shine. Without care and attention, the jewel remains buried in rough stone. Education, mentorship, and self-cultivation are essential.

### Yang Water 壬 (Im) — The Ocean

**Element:** Water | **Polarity:** Yang | **Image:** The vast ocean or a great river

Yang Water people are deep, powerful, and unstoppable. Like the ocean, they contain multitudes — calm on the surface, churning with currents below. They think big, dream big, and act on a scale that others find overwhelming.

- **Strengths:** Vision, ambition, adaptability, intelligence, resourcefulness
- **Challenges:** Restlessness, unreliability, scattered energy, emotional turbulence, difficulty with boundaries
- **In relationships:** Exciting and generous, but unpredictable — their moods shift like tides
- **Career inclinations:** International business, philosophy, travel, shipping, technology — fields without borders

Yang Water cannot be contained. Trying to control it only creates flooding. It needs channels (Earth) to direct its immense energy productively.

### Yin Water 癸 (Gye) — The Rain

**Element:** Water | **Polarity:** Yin | **Image:** Gentle rain or morning dew

Yin Water people are quiet, perceptive, and deeply intuitive. They nourish everything they touch, but subtly — you barely notice the rain until the flowers bloom. They are the observers, the thinkers, the ones who understand without being told.

- **Strengths:** Intuition, empathy, intelligence, gentleness, spiritual depth
- **Challenges:** Passivity, melancholy, overthinking, difficulty with self-assertion, tendency toward isolation
- **In relationships:** Deeply understanding and emotionally available, but can be withdrawn when hurt
- **Career inclinations:** Research, spirituality, writing, healing, data analysis — fields that require deep observation

Yin Water is the most yin of all Day Masters. It operates in the unseen realm — feelings, intuitions, undercurrents. It understands what cannot be put into words.

## Strong vs. Weak Day Master

Knowing your Day Master is the first step. The second — and equally important — step is assessing whether your Day Master is **strong** or **weak** in the context of your chart. This determination fundamentally changes how every other element is interpreted.

### What Makes a Day Master Strong?

A Day Master is considered strong when it receives significant support from the rest of the chart:

- **Same-element support.** If other stems or branches share your element, they strengthen you. A Yin Wood Day Master with Wood in other pillars has allies.
- **Resource element support.** The element that *produces* your Day Master strengthens it. Water produces Wood, so Water elements in a Wood Day Master's chart provide nourishment.
- **Seasonal strength.** If you were born in a season that matches your element, your Day Master gains strength. A Wood Day Master born in spring (Wood season) is naturally vital.
- **Root in branches.** If your Day Master's element appears in the Earthly Branches (especially as a Hidden Stem in the Day Branch), it has "roots" — a solid foundation.

### What Makes a Day Master Weak?

A Day Master is weak when it is drained, controlled, or unsupported:

- **Output drain.** Elements that your Day Master produces drain its energy. Wood produces Fire, so excessive Fire in a Wood chart exhausts the Day Master.
- **Wealth drain.** Elements that your Day Master controls also drain it — controlling takes effort. Wood controls Earth, so too much Earth tires a Wood Day Master.
- **Authority pressure.** Elements that control your Day Master suppress it. Metal controls Wood (the axe chops the tree), so heavy Metal presence pressures a Wood Day Master.
- **Seasonal weakness.** A Wood Day Master born in autumn (Metal season) faces a hostile environment.

### The Balance Principle

Here is the crucial insight: **strong is not better than weak, and weak is not worse than strong.** They simply change what the chart needs.

| Day Master Strength | What the Chart Needs | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| Strong | Output, Wealth, Authority — channels to release energy | Success through action, expression, and challenge |
| Weak | Resources, Companions — support to sustain energy | Success through collaboration, learning, and nurturing |

A strong Day Master with strong Authority elements in the chart produces a natural leader — someone who both has power and faces challenges that sharpen them. A weak Day Master with strong Resource elements produces a lifelong learner — someone who grows through mentorship, education, and support.

The **Useful God** — which we will study in Chapter 7 — is the specific element that best balances the chart based on this strong/weak assessment.

**Day Master:** 乙 (Yin Wood)

- Wood support: 甲 (Yang Wood) in Hour + 卯 (Rabbit, pure Wood) in Day Branch — strong roots
- Metal pressure: 庚 (Yang Metal) and 辛 (Yin Metal) in Year and Month stems — authority from both sides

**Assessment:** This is a moderately strong Day Master. The vine has deep roots (卯) and a fellow tree (甲) for support, plus Water nourishment from below. But it faces constant Metal pressure — authority, rules, and external demands from two directions. The Useful God is Wood — more support to withstand the Metal.

**Life pattern:** Chart A's owner started life with considerable strength. The Wood support gave her confidence and independence. But the Metal pressure meant she always operated under significant authority — whether from family expectations, institutional roles, or social obligations. Her Grand Fortune cycles shifted from supportive Wood periods in her youth to challenging Metal/Earth periods in her later years — hence the description "started strong, became weak."

- Fire drain: 丁 + 丙 + 丙 = three Fire elements draining Wood energy through output
- Earth drain: 丑 + 未 = two Earth branches, controlled by Wood (costs energy)
- Metal pressure: 申 (Monkey, Metal) in Month Branch
- Water nourishment: 子 (Rat, Water) in Year Branch — the only lifeline

**Assessment:** This is an extremely weak Day Master (*geuksinnyak*, 극신약). The vine has no roots, no allies, and is being drained from every direction — Fire is consuming it, Earth is demanding it, and Metal is cutting it. The only support is a single Water source in the Year Branch. The Useful God is also Wood — desperately needed support.

**Life pattern:** Chart B's owner is a profoundly creative person (three Fire elements = massive output energy), but she burns through her resources quickly. Without companions or support systems, she can feel isolated and overwhelmed. Her chart suggests someone who gives far more than she receives — talented and expressive, but at risk of exhaustion unless she consciously builds support structures.

### The Lesson

Same Day Master. Same element. Same polarity. But the surrounding elements create completely different lives:

| | Chart A (1960) | Chart B (1996) |
|---|---|---|
| **Day Master** | 乙 Yin Wood | 乙 Yin Wood |
| **Strength** | Moderate | Extremely weak |
| **Support** | Wood + Water present | Almost no support |
| **Main pressure** | Metal (authority) | Fire (output drain) |
| **Life theme** | Endurance under authority | Creative expression despite depletion |
| **Useful God** | Wood | Wood |

This is why Saju goes far beyond a simple ten-type system. The Day Master gives you the *character*. The surrounding elements give you the *story*.
