If the Four Pillars chart is a stage, the Day Master is the main character. Everything else — the other stems, the branches, the Hidden Stems, the fortune cycles — exists in relation to this one character. Understanding your Day Master is the first and most important step in reading any Saju chart.

In this chapter, you will meet all ten Day Masters and learn how to assess whether yours is strong or weak. Then we will see how two people with the same Day Master can live very different lives — proof that Saju is far more nuanced than a simple ten-type personality system.

## What Is the Day Master?

The **Day Master** — *Ilgan* (일간 日干) — is the Heavenly Stem of your Day Pillar. It is the single most important character in your entire chart.

Why the Day Pillar? Remember from Chapter 2 that Xu Ziping, the Song dynasty master who perfected the Four Pillars system, deliberately shifted the interpretive center from the Year Pillar to the Day Pillar. His reasoning was profound: the day you were born is the most *personal* unit of time. Your birth year is shared with millions of people. Your birth month is shared with hundreds of thousands. But your birth day, combined with your hour, narrows the cosmic signature down to something uniquely yours.

The Day Master represents three things. It is your fundamental nature, the instinctive way you respond to the world. It is your core identity, the self that remains constant even as circumstances change. And it is the reference point for everything else in the chart, because every other element is interpreted in terms of its relationship to your Day Master.

> Think of the Day Master as the sun in your personal solar system. The other elements are planets orbiting around it — sometimes supporting, sometimes challenging, but always defined by their relationship to the center.

## The 10 Day Masters: Complete Profiles

Each Day Master is defined by its element (Wood, Fire, Earth, Metal, Water) and its polarity (Yang or Yin). These two qualities together create ten distinct archetypes.

### Yang Wood 甲 (Gap) — The Tall Tree

Yang Wood carries the image of an ancient oak, standing tall and straight. Yang Wood people are natural leaders. Like a great tree, they grow upward, always reaching for something higher. They are principled, upright, and direct. When they believe in something, they stand firm, even when the wind pushes hard.

Integrity and leadership define this type. They are ambitious, straightforward, and possess a pioneering spirit that drives them to break new ground. At the same time, these qualities can harden into rigidity and stubbornness. Under pressure, Yang Wood struggles to compromise and may become inflexible when bending would serve them better.

In relationships, they are protective and loyal, though they can become controlling, wanting to shelter others under their branches. They gravitate toward management, entrepreneurship, law, politics, and architecture, fields that require vision and structural thinking.

Yang Wood needs sunlight (Fire) and water to grow. When supported, they become towering presences. When unsupported, they become brittle and can snap under pressure.

### Yin Wood 乙 (Eul) — The Vine

Yin Wood evokes the image of a climbing vine or a field of wildflowers. Yin Wood people are adaptable and resilient. Unlike the tall tree that breaks in a storm, the vine bends and finds another path. They are diplomatic, socially intelligent, and often underestimated, which they use to their advantage.

Flexibility and diplomacy are their greatest assets, along with emotional intelligence, persistence, and natural charm. Their challenges mirror these strengths: they can become indecisive, dependent on others, reluctant to stand alone, and prone to avoiding confrontation rather than facing it.

In relationships, they are warm, attentive, and accommodating, though they can become clingy or lose themselves in the other person. They do well in counseling, design, writing, teaching, and human resources, fields that reward connection and adaptability.

Yin Wood thrives when it has something to cling to, whether a strong partner, a supportive team, or a stable environment. In isolation, it struggles. But given any support at all, it grows in remarkable directions.

### Yang Fire 丙 (Byeong) — The Sun

Yang Fire carries the image of the blazing sun at midday. Yang Fire people light up every room they enter. They are warm, generous, optimistic, and impossible to ignore. Like the sun, they radiate energy outward, giving freely and attracting others naturally.

Charisma and natural warmth are their defining qualities, alongside generosity, optimism, and expressiveness. The shadow side is impulsiveness and difficulty with subtlety. They risk burnout and can overwhelm others with the sheer intensity of their presence.

In relationships, they are passionate, devoted, and demonstrative, though they can turn dramatic and need constant appreciation. They thrive in performance, media, public speaking, hospitality, and education, fields that put them in the spotlight.

Yang Fire does not discriminate. The sun shines on everyone equally, which makes them beloved but sometimes naive about who deserves their warmth.

### Yin Fire 丁 (Jeong) — The Candle

Yin Fire evokes a candle flame or a campfire. Yin Fire people have an intimate, focused warmth. Unlike the sun that illuminates everything, the candle lights only what is close. They are perceptive, intuitive, and deeply thoughtful, seeing details that others miss.

Perception and intuition are their core gifts, supported by focus, sensitivity, and intellectual depth. On the difficult side, they are prone to moodiness, overthinking, jealousy, and emotional intensity. Once attached to something, they struggle to let go.

In relationships, they are deeply devoted but possessive. Their love is like a flame that can warm or burn. They excel in research, psychology, technology, art, and detective work, fields that reward insight and focus.

Yin Fire needs fuel (Wood) to sustain itself. Without purpose or passion to feed on, the flame flickers and fades.

### Yang Earth 戊 (Mu) — The Mountain

Yang Earth carries the image of a great mountain, solid and unshakable. Yang Earth people are the most stable and reliable of all Day Masters. They are grounded, patient, and trustworthy. Like a mountain, they do not move for anyone, which makes them both a fortress and an obstacle.

Stability and reliability define this type, along with patience, tolerance, and deep groundedness. Their challenges are the reverse of their strengths: stubbornness, slowness to change, passivity, and a tendency to stagnate when left unchallenged.

In relationships, they are dependable and nurturing, but can seem emotionally flat. They show love through action, not words. They gravitate toward real estate, agriculture, construction, management, and government, fields that value consistency above all.

Yang Earth contains hidden treasures (Metal is born from Earth), but you have to mine deep to find them. They reveal their depth slowly.

### Yin Earth 己 (Gi) — The Garden Soil

Yin Earth evokes rich garden soil, fertile and nurturing. Yin Earth people are warm, accommodating, and productive. They provide the ground in which others grow. They are the caretakers, the supporters, the ones who make things possible behind the scenes.

Their nature is nurturing, practical, and modest. They are productive without calling attention to themselves. The downside is self-doubt and people-pleasing. They have difficulty saying no and tend to absorb others' problems as their own.

In relationships, they are devoted and selfless, but can lose their identity in the process, giving until there is nothing left. They find fulfillment in healthcare, the food industry, childcare, social work, and agriculture, fields that nurture growth in others.

Yin Earth is the most underestimated Day Master. They seem ordinary, but nothing grows without soil. Their value becomes apparent when they are absent.

### Yang Metal 庚 (Gyeong) — The Sword

Yang Metal carries the image of a forged sword or an axe. Yang Metal people are sharp, decisive, and action-oriented. They cut through problems with a directness that others find either refreshing or terrifying. They value justice, efficiency, and results.

Decisiveness and courage are their hallmarks, along with directness, a strong sense of justice, and fierce competitiveness. These same qualities can become harshness, insensitivity, and aggression. They struggle with nuance and sometimes create conflict where diplomacy would serve better.

In relationships, they are loyal and protective, but can be domineering, preferring to fix problems rather than simply listen. They excel in military service, surgery, finance, law enforcement, and engineering, fields that require precision and resolve.

Yang Metal must be tempered by Fire to become useful. Without challenges and refinement, the sword stays dull. Adversity literally makes them stronger.

### Yin Metal 辛 (Sin) — The Jewel

Yin Metal evokes a polished gemstone or fine jewelry. Yin Metal people are refined, aesthetic, and keenly sensitive. They notice quality in people, in environments, in ideas. They have exacting standards and a natural elegance that draws attention.

Refinement and aesthetic sense define this type, complemented by precision, eloquence, and exceptional attention to detail. Their challenges grow from the same roots: perfectionism, vanity, sensitivity to criticism, emotional fragility, and a demanding nature that others can find exhausting.

In relationships, they are romantic and attentive, but high-maintenance. They expect the same care they give. They are drawn to art, luxury goods, beauty, finance, and quality control, fields that demand aesthetic judgment.

Yin Metal needs to be polished to shine. Without care and attention, the jewel remains buried in rough stone. Education, mentorship, and self-cultivation are essential.

### Yang Water 壬 (Im) — The Ocean

Yang Water carries the image of the vast ocean or a great river. Yang Water people are deep, powerful, and unstoppable. Like the ocean, they contain multitudes, calm on the surface, churning with currents below. They think big, dream big, and act on a scale that others find overwhelming.

Vision and ambition are their driving forces, supported by adaptability, intelligence, and resourcefulness. Their difficulties stem from the same immensity: restlessness, unreliability, scattered energy, emotional turbulence, and a poor sense of boundaries.

In relationships, they are exciting and generous, but unpredictable. Their moods shift like tides. They thrive in international business, philosophy, travel, shipping, and technology, fields without borders.

Yang Water cannot be contained. Trying to control it only creates flooding. It needs channels (Earth) to direct its immense energy productively.

### Yin Water 癸 (Gye) — The Rain

Yin Water evokes gentle rain or morning dew. Yin Water people are quiet, perceptive, and deeply intuitive. They nourish everything they touch, but subtly. You barely notice the rain until the flowers bloom. They are the observers, the thinkers, the ones who understand without being told.

Intuition and empathy are their core qualities, alongside intelligence, gentleness, and spiritual depth. Their challenges include passivity, melancholy, overthinking, difficulty asserting themselves, and a tendency toward isolation.

In relationships, they are deeply understanding and emotionally available, but can withdraw when hurt. They find their calling in research, spirituality, writing, healing, and data analysis, fields that require deep observation.

Yin Water is the most yin of all Day Masters. It operates in the unseen realm of feelings, intuitions, and undercurrents. It understands what cannot be put into words.

## Strong vs. Weak Day Master

Knowing your Day Master is the first step. The second — and equally important — step is assessing whether your Day Master is **strong** or **weak** in the context of your chart. This determination fundamentally changes how every other element is interpreted.

### What Makes a Day Master Strong?

A Day Master is considered strong when it receives significant support from the rest of the chart.

Same-element support matters most directly. If other stems or branches share your element, they strengthen you. A Yin Wood Day Master with Wood in other pillars has allies. Resource element support also helps: the element that *produces* your Day Master strengthens it. Water produces Wood, so Water elements in a Wood Day Master's chart provide nourishment. Seasonal strength plays a role as well. If you were born in a season that matches your element, your Day Master gains strength. A Wood Day Master born in spring (Wood season) is naturally vital. Finally, root in branches provides a solid foundation. If your Day Master's element appears in the Earthly Branches (especially as a Hidden Stem in the Day Branch), it has deep roots to draw upon.

### What Makes a Day Master Weak?

A Day Master is weak when it is drained, controlled, or unsupported.

Output drain is one common cause. Elements that your Day Master produces drain its energy. Wood produces Fire, so excessive Fire in a Wood chart exhausts the Day Master. Wealth drain works similarly: elements that your Day Master controls also cost energy, because controlling takes effort. Wood controls Earth, so too much Earth tires a Wood Day Master. Authority pressure comes from elements that control your Day Master, suppressing it. Metal controls Wood (the axe chops the tree), so heavy Metal presence pressures a Wood Day Master. Seasonal weakness compounds these factors. A Wood Day Master born in autumn (Metal season) faces a hostile environment from the start.

### The Balance Principle

Here is the crucial insight: **strong is not better than weak, and weak is not worse than strong.** They simply change what the chart needs.

| Day Master Strength | What the Chart Needs | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| Strong | Output, Wealth, Authority — channels to release energy | Success through action, expression, and challenge |
| Weak | Resources, Companions — support to sustain energy | Success through collaboration, learning, and nurturing |

A strong Day Master with strong Authority elements in the chart produces a natural leader — someone who both has power and faces challenges that sharpen them. A weak Day Master with strong Resource elements produces a lifelong learner — someone who grows through mentorship, education, and support.

The **Useful God** — which we will study in Chapter 7 — is the specific element that best balances the chart based on this strong/weak assessment.

The Day Master is 乙 (Yin Wood). The chart landscape shows Wood support from 甲 (Yang Wood) in the Hour Pillar and 卯 (Rabbit, pure Wood) in the Day Branch, giving strong roots. Metal pressure comes from both 庚 (Yang Metal) and 辛 (Yin Metal) in the Year and Month stems, creating authority from both sides. This is a moderately strong Day Master. The vine has deep roots (卯) and a fellow tree (甲) for support, plus Water nourishment from below. But it faces constant Metal pressure, with authority, rules, and external demands pressing from two directions. The Useful God is Wood, providing more support to withstand the Metal.

Chart A's owner started life with considerable strength. The Wood support gave her confidence and independence. But the Metal pressure meant she always operated under significant authority, whether from family expectations, institutional roles, or social obligations. Her Grand Fortune cycles shifted from supportive Wood periods in her youth to challenging Metal/Earth periods in her later years, hence the description "started strong, became weak."

The vine has no tree to cling to. Fire drain is severe: 丁 + 丙 + 丙 gives three Fire elements draining Wood energy through output. This is an extremely weak Day Master (*geuksinnyak*, 극신약). The vine has no roots, no allies, and is being drained from every direction. Fire is consuming it, Earth is demanding it, and Metal is cutting it. The only support is a single Water source in the Year Branch. The Useful God is also Wood, representing desperately needed support.

Chart B's owner is a profoundly creative person (three Fire elements produce massive output energy), but she burns through her resources quickly. Without companions or support systems, she can feel isolated and overwhelmed. Her chart suggests someone who gives far more than she receives, talented and expressive, but at risk of exhaustion unless she consciously builds support structures.

### The Lesson

Same Day Master. Same element. Same polarity. But the surrounding elements create completely different lives.

| | Chart A (1960) | Chart B (1996) |
|---|---|---|
| **Day Master** | 乙 Yin Wood | 乙 Yin Wood |
| **Strength** | Moderate | Extremely weak |
| **Support** | Wood + Water present | Almost no support |
| **Main pressure** | Metal (authority) | Fire (output drain) |
| **Life theme** | Endurance under authority | Creative expression despite depletion |
| **Useful God** | Wood | Wood |

This is why Saju goes far beyond a simple ten-type system. The Day Master gives you the *character*. The surrounding elements give you the *story*.
