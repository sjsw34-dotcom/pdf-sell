# Compatibility Quick-Check

Here's the thing about compatibility in Saju — it's not about "good match" or "bad match." It's about understanding the *dynamic* between two people so you can work with it instead of against it.

In Saju, compatibility starts with the **element of each person's Day Master**. That's it. Two elements. One relationship dynamic.

Let's figure out yours.

## The Five Relationship Dynamics

Every element-to-element pairing falls into one of five categories. These are based on the **Productive Cycle** (elements that feed each other) and the **Controlling Cycle** (elements that check each other):

**Productive Cycle:** Wood → Fire → Earth → Metal → Water → Wood

**Controlling Cycle:** Wood → Earth → Water → Fire → Metal → Wood

From these two cycles, any pairing between your element and someone else's creates one of these dynamics:

- **Harmonious** — They produce you. Energy flows naturally toward you. You feel supported and nourished.
- **Supportive** — You produce them. You naturally give energy to this person. It feels generous but can be draining.
- **Neutral** — Same element. You understand each other deeply but may compete for the same space.
- **Tense** — You control them. You challenge this person, which can create growth or friction depending on awareness.
- **Challenging** — They control you. You feel pressured or pushed by this person. Growth is possible but requires effort.

### How to Read This Table

Find your element in the left column, then read across to your partner's element in the top row. The result describes **your experience** of the relationship.

- **You are Wood, Partner is Water** → Harmonious. Water feeds Wood — you feel nourished.
- **You are Wood, Partner is Earth** → Tense. Wood controls Earth — you push them, which can create friction.
- **You are Wood, Partner is Metal** → Challenging. Metal controls Wood — you feel pressured.

## What Each Dynamic Actually Feels Like

### Harmonious (They produce you)
They feed your energy without even trying. Conversations flow. You feel recharged after spending time together. The risk? You might take this person for granted because the support feels so effortless.

**Examples:** Water → Wood, Wood → Fire, Fire → Earth, Earth → Metal, Metal → Water

### Supportive (You produce them)
You naturally nurture, teach, or uplift this person. It feels good — you're the generous one. But over time, if the giving is one-directional, it can drain you. Healthy boundaries make this dynamic beautiful.

**Examples:** Wood → Fire, Fire → Earth, Earth → Metal, Metal → Water, Water → Wood

### Neutral (Same element)
You get each other. Same wavelength, same instincts, same blind spots. It's comfortable but can turn competitive — two fires in one room can either warm the house or burn it down. Respect each other's space.

**Examples:** Wood + Wood, Fire + Fire, Earth + Earth, Metal + Metal, Water + Water

### Tense (You control them)
You see their weaknesses clearly. You naturally push them to change or improve, which can feel like leadership or like criticism — depending on delivery. Great for mentorship when handled with care.

**Examples:** Wood → Earth, Fire → Metal, Earth → Water, Metal → Wood, Water → Fire

### Challenging (They control you)
This person pushes your buttons. They might not even mean to — it's just the energy. You feel tested, pressured, or called to level up. These relationships are the hardest but often the most transformative if both people are self-aware.

**Examples:** Earth → Wood, Metal → Fire, Water → Earth, Wood → Metal, Fire → Water
